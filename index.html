Are sleep disorders Disrupting Your Restful Nights?

Sleep disorders affect many aspects of everyday life, but they can be significantly improved by early detection and treatment. If you want to enhance your quality of life and sleep, you must be aware of these conditions and seek the appropriate help. More than 80 conditions are classified as sleep disorders, and they affect the length, timing, and quality of sleep, among other factors. These conditions affect your ability to fall asleep, stay asleep for extended periods of time, and wake up how much. If you regularly have problems falling asleep, wake up feeling exhausted even after seven hours of sleep, or have trouble with everyday tasks, you may have a sleep disorder. Based on symptoms and how they affect people, the International Classification of Sleep Disorders (ICSD) updates its classifications on a regular basis. Among the 80 varieties, obstructive sleep apnea (difficulty breathing during sleep), restless legs syndrome, and chronic insomnia are some common disorders.
